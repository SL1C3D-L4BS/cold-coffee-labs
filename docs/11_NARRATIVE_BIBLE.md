## 1. Cosmology — The Primordial Schism

### 1.1 The Absolute

Before creation there was **the Absolute**: undifferentiated unity, neither being nor non-being. The Absolute was not a god. It was the precondition for gods.

The Absolute fractured. Not because it chose to; because unity is unstable. The fracture produced three aspects, each containing the other two in shadow.

### 1.2 The One

**The One** is the God of mercy, justice, and stewardship. The author of Heaven, the codifier of the commandments, the speaker of the first word. The One chose silence at the moment the first human committed the first irrevocable act. The silence was not abandonment — the One believes silence is the highest form of mercy. Humanity has interpreted it as every other thing.

The silence is the game's starting condition.

### 1.3 The Other

**The Other** is the adversary: the jealous shadow of the One. The Other authored Hell, not as a punishment but as a **recycler** — a factory that breaks down damned souls and reforges them as weapons, monsters, and the structure of Hell itself. The LEGION (§3) is the Other's workforce. The Factory Floor (§4) is the Other's cathedral.

The Other is not the villain of *Sacrilege*. The Other is honest; the Other admits what it is.

### 1.4 The Witness

**The Witness** is the third aspect: neutral, older than both siblings, older in feel than the Absolute itself. The Witness authored the **Silentium**, a mirror dimension where sound cannot travel and light travels inverted. The Witness watches. The Witness walks. The Witness speaks exactly once in the game — the epilogue of the Grace ending (§6).

The Witness is the Bible's theological answer to the problem of silence: silence is not absence, silence is the third voice.

## 2. The Protagonist — Malakor / Father Niccolò Bianchi

### 2.1 Father Niccolò Bianchi

A Vatican exorcist, Italian, early fifties, widowed, lost a child in the year before the game opens. Niccolò is the **moral voice**: measured, regretful, devotional, fluent in Latin and doubt. He has performed one hundred and seventy-three exorcisms. Most did nothing. Three worked. He cannot explain why.

Niccolò commits the ritual the CORE has forbidden since the First Council: the *Rite of Unspeaking*, which fuses a human soul with a fallen angel. He commits it because his silence has become unbearable — the One's silence, his wife's silence, his own. He fuses himself with Malakor to **force a response**.

### 2.2 Malakor

A fallen angel of the **Unwritten** — the angelic faction that defected from the One's silence. Malakor is the **combat voice**: aggressive, hungry, amoral, profane, ecstatic in violence. Malakor is older than the Church, older than Rome, older than the language either of them speak.

Malakor is not evil. Malakor is **unwritten** — free of the One's codification. Malakor's morality is not absent; it is not the One's.

### 2.3 The Duality

The fusion is not possession. The player is **both** at once. The voice director (§5) selects who speaks based on combat state and play-style. Never both speak at once. Both hear each other.

- Niccolò can surface during God Mode and be ignored; Malakor cannot surface during Ruin and is silenced.
- When the player reaches 100 Grace, Niccolò speaks with Malakor's authority for the first time. This is the climactic line: *"We forgive."*

The player never sees Niccolò's face. The first-person view, the voice, the argument in the head — that is the character.

## 3. The Factions

### 3.1 The CORE — Church of the Recycled Eden

The institutional antagonist. A consolidated Vatican-plus-megachurch bureaucracy that has, for seven centuries, **manufactured the One's response** for political control. The CORE's clerics speak as if they channel the One; they do not. The cyber-cherubim (Cherubim enemy, `docs/07` §5) are the CORE's industrial angels.

The CORE's sin is not evil. It is **counterfeit mercy**. The CORE believes humanity cannot survive the One's silence and has to be lied to. The CORE may be right.

Factional kills: Mantra-rich. Dialogue: Niccolò is the one who recognizes every face.

### 3.2 The LEGION

Hell's infrastructure. The Other's workforce. Soul-recyclers. The LEGION has no ideology — the LEGION has **throughput**. Deacons, Martyrs, Hell Knights, Painweavers are all LEGION uniforms; the soul underneath was once a person the CORE couldn't save.

Factional kills: Sin-rich. Dialogue: Malakor is the one who knows the Factory.

### 3.3 The UNWRITTEN

Fallen angels who defected from the One's silence. Malakor's people. They are rare. They are mostly silent. They are hiding. The player encounters them in the Sewer of Grace (Circle VIII) and can **choose not to kill them**. Grace accrues. See `docs/07` §2.7.

The Unwritten are not allies in the combat sense. They are the Bible's argument that silence is not the only response to the One's silence; *defection* is also a response.

## 5. The Three Acts

### 5.1 Act I — *The Rite of Unspeaking*

Niccolò performs the fusion. The player learns Sin, Mantra, movement. First Rapture (auto-triggered) is the first **God Mode** beat; the narrative hook fires — Niccolò crying, Malakor laughing. The player crosses Rome, the Vatican Necropolis, and into the Sewer of Grace. Tutorial-dense; Blasphemies unlock one per Circle.

Circles: I, II, III. Ends when the player first triggers *Rapture* and survives it.

### 5.2 Act II — *The Blasphemy Circuit*

The player carves through the CORE (Vatican → Palazzo of the Unspoken → Colosseum of Echoes) and into the LEGION (Hell's Factory Floor). Full Blasphemy economy. Sin-signature (§6.1) begins driving dialogue divergence. The **Witness** is glimpsed once at the end of Circle VI; no dialogue yet.

Circles: IV, V, VI, VII. Ends when the player completes the Colosseum encounter.

### 5.3 Act III — *The Unmaking*

The player descends through Fraud and Treachery. The Sewer of Grace reveals the Unwritten; restraint choices accumulate Grace. The Silentium mirror-profile is entered. The Logos fight — the "God Machine" — begins. Phase 4 branches:

- **Annihilation ending** — Malakor wins. The One remains silent. Niccolò is voiceless in the epilogue.
- **Grace ending** — the player spends 100 Sin + 100 Grace on the `forgive` Blasphemy. The Witness speaks. The game ends in white.

Circles: VIII, IX + Silentium. See `docs/07` §7b for the Phase-4 selector details.

## 6. Narrative Reactivity

### 6.1 The Sin-Signature

A deterministic five-channel play-style fingerprint. See `docs/07` §15b.1 for the channels and `docs/06` §3.33 for the engine module (`engine/narrative/sin_signature.hpp`). Every line the voice director picks is a function of Sin-signature × Act × Circle × encounter × seed. No runtime randomness.

| Signature pattern | Narrative tint |
|-------------------|----------------|
| High cruelty, low precision | Malakor ecstatic, Niccolò stunned |
| High precision, low cruelty | Niccolò measured, Malakor impatient |
| High God-Mode uptime | Malakor dominates; Niccolò rare |
| High hitless streak | Both voices converge on awe |
| High deaths/area | Both voices drop to silence; environment carries the beat |

### 6.2 The Voice Director

See `docs/06` §3.33 and `docs/07` §15b.2. Production rules:

- **God Mode** → Malakor only.
- **Ruin** → Niccolò only.
- **Combat idle** → weighted roll by Sin-signature.
- **Exploration** → Niccolò-biased.
- **Grace window** (Phase-4) → the *We forgive* line, both voices, the only time they speak together.

Every line is authored offline, HITL-approved through `bld-governance`, cooked to `.gwdlg`.

### 6.3 The Grace Finale

See `docs/07` §15b.3. The Witness's single line is chosen deterministically from three authored variants based on Sin-signature at game-end:

- *"You forgave because you were tired, not because you were merciful."* — high cruelty, low precision.
- *"You forgave because you understood the silence."* — high precision, low cruelty.
- *"You forgave. The word is enough."* — balanced.

All three are the Bible's three theses about mercy. None is framed as correct.

## 7. Franchise Future

### 7.1 *Sacrilege 2: Apostasy*

Post-Grace world. The One speaks — and what the One says is worse than the silence. The player is a second Unwritten, fused not with a priest but with a **child**. The mechanics inherit the Martyrdom Engine; the narrative inverts the Grace finale: the terminal Blasphemy is `abandon`, not `forgive`.

### 7.2 *Sacrilege: Silentium*

A slower, horror-forward spin-off set entirely in the Silentium mirror dimension. The Witness is the narrator. No voice director — the Witness is the only voice. Movement is slower; sound is inverted. The Witness game.

### 7.3 *Sacrilege: The Witness*

A non-first-person title. The player is the Witness across all three siblings' domains. Concept stage.

### 7.4 Non-game Media

Audio drama, a single graphic novel (*The Unwritten*), and one limited-run physical artefact (Sin/Grace counters) in the art-book edition.

## 8. Forbidden Narrative Moves

This is the short list of narrative choices the Bible prohibits. Anyone proposing one needs an ADR override:

1. **No redemption of the CORE.** The CORE remains institutionally false. Individual clerics can be sympathetic.
2. **No demonization of the LEGION.** They are workers, not villains.
3. **No "it was all a dream."** The fusion is real. The silence is real.
4. **No on-screen Niccolò.** First-person only. Voice only.
5. **No runtime LLM narration.** All lines authored, HITL-approved, pinned.
6. **No "evil Niccolò / good Malakor" flip.** Both characters contain both poles at all times.
7. **No franchise retcons that invalidate a speedrun category.** The seed is canon.
